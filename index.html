About Me

Brent Nicholas is a Computer Science Graduate, Software Engineer, Aircraft mechanic, and US Army veteran offering a wide array of technical skills. In 2012, Brent enlisted in the Texas Army National Guard as an Aircraft Pneudraulics Repairer. That next year, 2013, Brent graduated from Fort Rucker, Alabama at the top of his class and received the Distinguished Service Graduate Award, an award that only two can receive out of a few hundred soldiers. In May 2014, Brent went on to graduate from Lone Star College - University Park and received his Associate's of Science. From early 2017 to early 2018, Brent left the States to serve his country in Kuwait. After coming back to the States, Brent went on to finish completing his Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science in December 2019.

Detailed oriented and highly motivated professional with several years of experience in Software Development, Computer Hardware Troubleshooting, implementing Data Structures and Algorithms, Networking, Packet Sniffing, Web Services, Game Engines, Continuos Integration, Command Line Interface, Digital Imaging, Linux and much more. Driven passion for getting things done and done right in a timely manner. Eager to learn new things with personal interests that are far and wide. Whether it's building databases, working with the front-end or back-end, implementing AI into drones, network testing, building applications, or working with embedded os devices I become highly interested and motivated in the work or project.

Image Blending

To illustrate the blending of images. Using a blending algorithm and adding an alpha channel to an image one can blend a smaller image on top of a larger image. The X and Y coordinates can be any coordinates whether they make it on or off the background image, though the Alpha needs to be in range of 0 to 1. 0, being no transparency and 1 being full transparency.

Photo Filter App

Allows user to upload an image and apply an array of filters to their image. This application utilizes the Butterworth Low/High, Gaussian Low/High, and Ideal Low/High frequency algorithms. More filtering algorithms will be coming soon, some to include will be the Olympic and Homomorphic frequencies. Another update that is in the making is peak signal-to-noise ratio (PSNR), if checked this will allow the computer to compute all the filters and select a suggested filter configuration for the best image quality of a given filter.
